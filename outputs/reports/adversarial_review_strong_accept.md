## 当前水平

WEAK-ACCEPT (borderline), with an asymmetric profile. Consensus across all three reviewers: the paper CLEARS 中科院二区 / a solid domain venue on the strength of unusual honesty and above-norm rigor (val-selected δ, honestly-downgraded LightGBM parity that reproduces per-seed, Holm+Wilson+HUC2 cluster bootstrap, κ negative result strengthened to 0% of stations, PICP reproduces 0.904/0.909/0.911, 166-artifact sha256 manifest, real-assertion tests). It is NOT strong-accept and is BELOW the CCF-A-conference / 一区-methodology bar. The gating reason is singular and shared: the honest headline collapses to "in-sample PARITY with a single global LightGBM," and the two axes that could rescue that into a contribution (spatial transfer; a distinctive mechanism) are both currently unsupported — transfer is (a) never benchmarked against LightGBM [F1] and (b) a random station split, not leave-region-out [H1]; the one distinctive mechanism (dynamic κ) is self-retracted [F2/H4]. Engineering lens (NVIDIA) independently lands at "accept, not strong-accept" because the reproducibility chain certifies one frozen non-deterministic run rather than a re-derivable result [E1-E4]. Net: strong on integrity and instrumentation, thin on defended novel advance.

## 核心张力(最关键)

BRUTALLY HONEST VERDICT: As-is, the contribution is NOT strong-accept-worthy, and the reason is structural, not cosmetic. Strip away what the paper itself honestly retracts and you are left with: "a physics-biased forecaster that TIES an off-the-shelf LightGBM in-sample, beats trivial persistence/damped and a self-crippled air2stream, and ships well-calibrated CQR intervals." Every reviewer converges here. Parity with a strong baseline is a fine HONEST result but it is not a CONTRIBUTION at an A-venue unless one of three things is true, and right now none is: (1) TRANSFER — a genuine out-of-distribution advantage over the strong learned baseline. This is the single highest-leverage lever and it is doubly broken: LightGBM is never run under leave-group-out at all [F1, MIT], and the "unseen basins" holdout is a RANDOM station partition where 11-14 of ~13 HUC2 regions and 7/8 nested same-river gage families straddle train/test [H1, Stanford] — verified: 13_rigor.py:99-102 is `rng.permutation`, and kfold_lgo() instantiates ONLY ThermoRoute. So the headline "+0.17/+0.17/+0.24 on unseen basins" is (a) spatially leaked and (b) only ever compared to persistence/damped, which a global LightGBM would also beat. (2) MECHANISM — a validated, generalizable physical/architectural insight. The intended one (flow/season-modulated dynamic κ, "dynamic thermal memory") is self-retracted (rises with flow at 0% of stations; freezing it is within-noise), leaving the "physics prior" functioning empirically as a damped-persistence-toward-climatology regularizer with a bounded residual — assembled from off-the-shelf primitives (sparsemax router, TCN, softmax MoE, tanh clamp) [F2/F5 MIT, H4 Stanford]. (3) METHOD — a novel calibration/uncertainty result. Calibration is textbook split-CQR applied correctly; the paper diagnoses non-exchangeability (2018→2019-2020) but never deploys the adaptive tools built for it (ACI/weighted-CQR/EnbPI) [F4 MIT]. WHAT WOULD FLIP IT: The cheapest, sharpest, highest-probability win is lever (1) done RIGHT — run BOTH LightGBM (global AND per-station) AND ThermoRoute under a genuine leave-HUC2-region-out / leave-river-out protocol (huc2 column already exists), with a per-station paired Wilcoxon/DM test. Two outcomes, both publishable: (A) if TR transfers SIGNIFICANTLY better than LightGBM out-of-region → this becomes a sharp, defensible central claim ("ties in-sample, generalizes better to ungauged basins") and plausibly reaches strong-accept; (B) if it ties out-of-region too → report it honestly and pivot the paper's identity to "calibrated-uncertainty-at-scale + an honest delineation of WHEN physics-biasing beats a GBDT," which is a legitimate 二区-strong / borderline-一区 methods-and-benchmark contribution but NOT an A-venue-novelty contribution. The decision the authors must make is which paper they are writing; the current draft hedges between the two and therefore fully satisfies neither. My assessment: outcome (A) is plausible but NOT likely — a station-agnostic NN and a global GBDT usually generalize comparably out-of-basin — so authors should run the LGO-vs-LGB experiment FIRST as a go/no-go gate before investing in the strong-accept framing, and be prepared to commit to framing (B).
